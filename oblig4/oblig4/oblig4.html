When a map is not a map

Created by: Sondre, Gillian and Natalie
Published: 29. oct. 10:00 Picture by Abed Ismail. Free to use under the Unsplash License

Anette (36) from Bærum is a full-time student who lives in Halden. In 2023, she will be a map reader in the rally race Africa Eco Tour, which runs from Monaco in southern Europe all the way to Dakar in West Africa.

When Anette was 5 years old, her dad came home with a "crosser" that was a tad big that he had bought as a birthday present in Belgium.

– Map reader does 50% of the work if not more. The fact that I navigate us correctly is so important.

Christina Dobloug is the rally driver who will be behind the wheel the more than 3000 kilometres it will take to get all the way to Dakar in Senegal.

– We must be very cohesive: it is not certain that you have time to say full words. If you have a turn combination of right and then left, you may just have to say "hø" and then you will say "ve". We’re talking about speeds at 200 kph!

There are not that many people that are in the African Eco Race. It is very special that there is a pure women's car, and it has never happened before from Norway. So, we must be role models and show that this is possible! This is possible!

When Anette was only 3-4 years old, her brother watched motocross on TV.

How did it start for you as a 5-year-old? What caught your attention with motorsports?

– My brother. I have a big brother who is 7 years older than me. He had seen motocross on TV (mopeds). That made him want to try it. Then dad found a local motorsport club in Bærum, and my brother got his own crosser. And then I was with them and watched as he trained. At the time I was probably 3-4 years old, and standing and pulling my dad in a sweater and saying, "I want to drive too!". I was very active as a child. Happily, hung upside down in the curtain and was a bit like a monkey.

In 2001 Anette dislocated her shoulder. However, that did not stop her from participating in competitions.

– In the European Championships in 2002 Oslo, I got 2nd place. Ola was one of Norway's best several times. Andreas was a member of the club who drove a lot, but never reached the top.

In 2004, Annette had to have surgery on her shoulder. She thus lost her professional contract.

The helmet looked like it was going to stay on the shelf.

What does it mean for you to be a role model?

– I had no starting point, I myself felt that I would never get to this point, or higher education for that matter!

– So, I hope that I can be a role model for others who feel that "No, I cannot do this", no matter what it should be! Because yes: you can do this!

What have you learned?

– I have learned that what I say to myself is very important. If I have wanted to do something and not do it then… I was very good at finding excuses why I shouldn’t take that jog. Or why I did not need higher education, or why it did not go so well with that race at the time.

– Having faith in oneself too. Keep a straight back and be a little proud of what you have managed to accomplish. For some it may be just getting up one day, while for others it may be carrying out a desert rally.
